Client:
  • Authorized APs: APs permitted in the WLAN, including APs connected to the AC and not in the prohibited device list and configured authorized APs.
  • Rogue APs: APs that cannot be used in the WLAN, including APs in the prohibited device list, APs not in the OUI configuration file, and configured rogue APs.
  • Misconfigured APs: APs that can be used in the WLAN but have incorrect configuration.
  • External APs: APs that are in an adjacent WLAN.
  • Ad hoc APs: APs in Ad hoc mode. WIPS detects Ad hoc APs by listening to beacon frames.
  • Potential-authorized APs: APs that are not in the permitted device list, the trusted OUI list, and the prohibited device list.
  • Potential-rogue APs: APs that are not in the permitted device list, the trusted OUI list, and the prohibited device list, but have incorrect configuration. The wired port of a potential-rogue AP is connected to the WLAN.
  • Potential-external APs: APs that are not in the permitted device list, the trusted OUI list, and the prohibited device list, but have incorrect configuration. The wired port of a potential-external AP is not connected to the WLAN.
  • Uncategorized APs: APs whose categories cannot be determined.
  • Authorized clients: Clients that are permitted in the WLAN, including clients in the permitted device list and associated with authorized APs and clients that have passed authentication and are associated with authorized APs.
  • Unauthorized clients: Clients that cannot be used in the WLAN, including clients in the prohibited device list, clients associated with rogue APs, and clients not in the OUI configuration file.
  • Misassociated clients: Clients in the permitted device list but associated with unauthorized APs. A misassociated client might introduce security threats to the network.
  • Uncategorized clients: Clients whose categories cannot be determined.
